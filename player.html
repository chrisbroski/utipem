Movement and Distance

  1. Far away - So far away that nothing can be seen (At another location, somewhere else, back at the ranch, etc.)
  2. Distant - Large objects can be seen without detail (In the distance, across the way, just outside, etc.)
  3. Medium - People can be seen clearly, but not interacted with without yelling or throwing something. (In the room, within earshot, etc.)
  4. Close - At a comfortable talking distance. A quick movement can put the two in contact.
  5. Engaged - Things are in contact, wrestling, whispering, etc.

One turn of movement can change the distance between you and a target by one level, with the exception of far away. That will depend on how far away you are.

It also takes one movement turn to stand from lying or sitting on the ground.

Moving or attempting a skill that targets another while engaged grants the engaged character one free skill attempt targetting you.

Turn Actions

On your turn, you may choose one action:

  1. Move
  2. Attempt a skill
  3. Pass the turn

Attempting a Skill

Any skill that you meet the attempt prerequisite for may be attempted.

Outcome is usually dependent on the result of rolling two 6-sided dice

Training

Training a skill is increasing its expertise level. Any skill may be trained if you meet its training prerequisite.

Dependent Skills

Some skills have a training prerequisite. It is most often intermediate expertise in another skill.

Fast action

Simple actions don't require and entire turn. A character may also do up to two of these types of actions in a turn.

  • Talk
  • Drop something they are holding
  • Stand up from a chair
  • Draw a weapon
  • Drop to the ground

Skill Expertise

Untrained

Any character may attempt any skill at this level as long as they have the attempt prerequisites.

Trained

A character must receive training or have succeeded once at an untrained level.

Intermediate

A character must succeed once at a trained skill.

Professional

For a skill to reach professional level of expertise, there must be a level II skill that is directly dependent on it reach intermediate level.

Expert

An expert skill must have at least two intermediated level dependent skills, one of which must be at least professional level.

Master

To master a skill, a character must have at least three intermediated level dependent skills, one of which must be at least expert level.

Combat Injuries

⚀ Near Miss

Consider yourself very lucky.

⚁ 'Tis but a scratch

You call that an injury?

⚂ Flesh Wound

If immediate medically attention is not successful, 24 hours later d6 < 3, wound elevates to #4.

⚃ That's Gonna Leave a Mark

If medically attention is not successfully given, 24 hours later d6 < 4, wound elevates to #5.

Head: All skills -1 for 7 days. Knocked out.

Chest: All skills -1 for 7 days. Knocked down.

Arm: All skills requiring arm at -1 for 7 days.

Leg: All movement skill -1 for 7 days.

⚄ Mortally wounded

Death occurs if medical attention not successful.

Inured anatomy unusable for 7 days.

⚅ Maximum

If head, chest or abdomen, character is dead.

If an appendage, limb is lost.

Effects

Knocked down

Character is on the ground and will require a move turn to stand up.

Knocked out

Character passes out for one turn and wakes up on the ground.
